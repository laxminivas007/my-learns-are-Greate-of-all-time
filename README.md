   <h2>Network Engineering</h2>
   <h3>TCP/IP & The Packet Lifecycle</h3>
   <p>To troubleshoot effectively, you must understand how data is chunked, numbered, and acknowledged. You need to know the exact mechanics of the three-way handshake and connection termination.
   What to master: SYN/ACK flags, sequence numbers, window scaling, and the concept of TCP states (TIME_WAIT, CLOSE_WAIT)</p>
   <a href=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/TCP/IP_Illustrated"">TCP/IP Illustrated, Volume 1: The Protocols</a>
   <p>This is the definitive, foundational text. Read the chapters on IP, TCP, and UDP</p>
   <a href="Julia Evans' Networking Zines:">Julia Evans' Networking Zines:</a>
   <p> Brilliantly simple, highly visual breakdowns of how packets actually move. Great for quick intuition.</p>
   <h3>DNS Resolution</h3>
   <p>"It's always DNS." In distributed microservice environments, service discovery relies entirely on DNS. You need to know exactly how a hostname translates to an IP address.
      What to master: The difference between recursive and authoritative nameservers, TTL (Time to Live), A/AAAA records, CNAMEs, and how to use dig to trace a resolution path.</p>
   <a href="https://www.cloudflare.com/learning/dns/what-is-dns/">Cloudflare's DNS Learning Center</a>
   <p> Exceptional, easy-to-read explanations of the entire DNS hierarchy.</p>
   <a href="https://howdns.works/">Julia Evans' "How DNS Works" Comic</a>
   <p>A fantastic, step-by-step visual guide to the resolution journey.</p>
   <h3>BGP Routing (The Internet's Map)</h3>
   <p>While you might not configure BGP directly every day, understanding it is crucial for cloud architecture and knowing how traffic reaches your edge load balancers.
      What to master: Autonomous Systems (AS), prefix advertising, and why routing anomalies (like BGP hijacking or flapping) cause massive outages.</p>
   <a href="https://www.cloudflare.com/learning/security/glossary/what-is-bgp/">Cloudflare: What is BGP?: </a>
   <p>Cloudflare: What is BGP?: A clear, concise breakdown of the "postal service" of the internet.</p>
   <a href="https://www.google.com/search?q=https://www.youtube.com/playlist%3Flist%3DPL2Z22CziV0I1Gq-1p00w4Zc1Ww2mO9nL7">Network Collective BGP Fundamentals: </a>
   <p>Excellent video series for real-world architectural context.</p>
   <h3>TLS Handshakes (Security & Latency)</h3>
   <p>When integrating APIs or securing internal cluster communication, TLS errors are the most common roadblock. You need to understand the cryptographic exchange that happens before the first byte of application data is sent.
      What to master: Certificate authorities, SNI (Server Name Indication), cipher suites, and the critical latency differences between TLS 1.2 and TLS 1.3.</p>
   <a href="https://tls13.xargs.org/">The Illustrated TLS 1.3 Connection:</a>
   <p> An absolute masterpiece. It gives you an interactive, byte-by-byte breakdown of a modern TLS handshake.</p>
   <a href="https://hpbn.co/transport-layer-security-tls/">High Performance Browser Networking (TLS Chapter):</a>
   <p> Deep dive into the performance implications of TLS handshakes.</p>
   <h3>The "Truth" Tool: tcpdump</h3>
   <p>When microservices stop communicating, logs can be misleading. Packets never lie. tcpdump is how you prove exactly where a connection is dying.
      What to master: Capturing traffic on specific interfaces, filtering by port/IP, saving to .pcap files, and filtering by TCP flags (e.g., catching a server sending a RST packet instead of a SYN-ACK).</p>
   <a href="https://danielmiessler.com/study/tcpdump/">Daniel Miessler's tcpdump Primer</a>
   <p>: One of the best, most practical cheat sheets for real-world usage.</p>
   <a href="https://www.wireshark.org/docs/">Wireshark documentation:</a>
   <p> Wireshark is the GUI equivalent. Learn to run tcpdump on the headless server, export the .pcap file, and analyze it visually in Wireshark on your local machine.</p>
